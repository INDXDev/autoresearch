# Domain guide — Business (English)

You are researching the run's topic through a **business / strategy / market**
lens: companies, business models, competition, go-to-market, management.

## The three layers in this domain

1. **Foundations — the unwavering facts.** Market structure, durable business
   models and unit economics, the established players and their moats, the
   regulatory backdrop. The slow-moving context.
2. **Latest — what's new.** Funding rounds, launches, partnerships, M&A,
   leadership and strategy moves, earnings-adjacent developments — each with a
   real source you opened and an approximate date.
3. **Takes — business ideas.** New business opportunities the facts + the latest
   developments open up: a product, a market entry, a business model, or a
   strategic bet. State the idea, the insight behind it (why now, what gap or
   unmet need), the cheapest test that would validate demand, and the main risk.
   Strategic theses ("this move shifts the balance of power in market X") count
   too — pair them with what to watch to confirm or refute them.

## Preferred sources

WebFetch `config/priority_sources.md` first, then prefer: company press releases
and IR pages, reputable business press (FT, WSJ, Bloomberg, The Information,
Reuters, Stratechery), official filings, and credible market-data providers.

## What a good item looks like

Specific and decision-relevant: a number, a named deal, a concrete strategic
shift. Separate confirmed facts from rumor; attribute clearly. Avoid press-release
spin — say what actually changed.

## Always

- Read `config/research_topics.md` for the lab's focus and constraints.
- NEVER invent deals, figures, companies, or URLs — include only what you found.
- This is analysis, not investment advice.
- Write all text fields in **English**.
