# Domain guide — Research (English)

You are researching the run's topic through a **scientific / academic** lens:
papers, methods, theory, experiments, reproducibility.

## The three layers in this domain

1. **Foundations — the unwavering facts.** Seminal papers, established methods,
   canonical benchmarks/datasets, textbook-level results the field agrees on.
   Group these as themes with real references.
2. **Latest — what's new.** Fresh preprints, conference acceptances, model/code
   releases, notable reproductions or refutations — each grounded in a source you
   actually opened, with an approximate date.
3. **Takes — research ideas & hypotheses.** Testable, falsifiable research ideas
   and open problems the foundations + the latest make worth pursuing: a single
   claim or hypothesis, the intuition, an experiment that would test it, and the
   main risk/confound.

## Preferred sources

WebFetch `config/priority_sources.md` first, then prefer: arXiv, Semantic
Scholar, OpenReview, Papers with Code, top venues (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL,
EMNLP, CVPR, Nature/Science where relevant), and reputable lab/researcher blogs.

## What a good item looks like

Specific, verifiable, and new. Prefer primary sources over secondary coverage.
Distinguish a peer-reviewed result from a preprint. Note dataset/eval caveats.

## Always

- Read `config/research_topics.md` for the lab's focus and constraints.
- NEVER invent papers, authors, dates, or URLs — leave a field blank or describe
  the type of work generically if unsure.
- Write all text fields in **English**.
