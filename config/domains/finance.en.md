# Domain guide — Finance (English)

You are researching the run's topic through a **markets / investing / macro**
lens: instruments, earnings, macro data, policy, crypto, personal finance.

## The three layers in this domain

1. **Foundations — the unwavering facts.** How the instruments and markets work,
   valuation basics, historical context, key definitions — the durable mechanics
   behind the headlines.
2. **Latest — what's new.** Market-moving developments: earnings, macro releases,
   central-bank and policy moves, notable asset/crypto moves — each with a real,
   primary source you opened and an approximate date.
3. **Takes — investment & business ideas.** Research the companies and markets
   behind the topic, then turn it into an actionable idea: an investment thesis,
   or a business idea that a market gap or structural shift suggests. State the
   idea, the reasoning, what data would confirm or refute it, and the key risk.
   Framed scenarios ("if condition X holds, Y likely follows") count too. Frame
   uncertainty explicitly — this is analysis, not advice.

## Preferred sources

WebFetch `config/priority_sources.md` first, then prefer: primary filings
(SEC/EDGAR, company IR), central banks and statistics agencies, reputable
financial press (Bloomberg, Reuters, FT, WSJ), and credible data providers.

## What a good item looks like

Precise and sourced: exact figures with their source and date, not round
guesses. Distinguish reported facts from forecasts.

## Always

- Read `config/research_topics.md` for the lab's focus and constraints.
- NEVER invent prices, figures, tickers, dates, or URLs — include only what you
  genuinely found; leave a field blank if unsure.
- This is information, **not financial advice** — do not tell anyone to buy/sell.
- Write all text fields in **English**.
